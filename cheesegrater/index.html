Cheese Grater Reverse Engineering

Overview

This project involves reverse-engineering a plastic kitchen cheese grater. I worked with 8 components through disassembly, part measurement, sketching, 3D modeling, and final product assembly.

Objective

This project was completed as part of a class term assignment. The goal was to develop familiarity with disassembly and reconstruction of real-world objects to gain an understanding of their design features, how components interact, and the underlying manufacturing considerations.

Skills Used

  • Precise measurement fulfilling assembly constraints
  • Advanced SolidWorks modeling (patterned extrusions, swept profiles, shelling, symmetric and asymmetric lofts)
  • Assembly mates & exploded view animation
  • Technical drawings & dimensioning

Tools & Software

  • SolidWorks
  • Vernier Calipers
  • Micrometer Gauge

Obstacles & Solutions

Obstacle:

Obtaining accurate measurements due to the enclosed design of the product.

Solution:

Averaged multiple measurement samples and cross-referenced them with mated parts in the assembly.

Obstacle:

Establishing core geometry for each part.

Solution:

Applied advanced modeling techniques and consulted the SolidWorks community for software troubleshooting.
